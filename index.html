Foundational Concepts

Translational Movement

Motion where an object shifts from one point to another without changing its orientation.
Example: A car on a straight road.

Rotational Movement

Motion where an object spins around a fixed axis, changing its orientation.
Example: A spinning planet.

Linear Displacement (s)

The shortest path and direction between two points. A vector measured in meters (m).

Angular Displacement (θ)

The angle an object rotates around an axis. A vector measured in radians (rad).

Dynamics: Forces in Motion

Vector & Acceleration

A vector has magnitude and direction. Acceleration (a) is the rate of change of velocity.

a = Δv / Δt

Mass (m)

A measure of matter and inertia, measured in kilograms (kg).

Force (F)

A push or pull that causes acceleration.

F = ma

Kinematics: Equations of Motion

These equations describe the motion of objects with constant acceleration.

Velocity-Time

v = v₀ + at

Final velocity (v) from initial velocity (v₀), acceleration (a), and time (t).

Displacement-Time

s = v₀t + ½at²

Displacement (s) from initial velocity, time, and acceleration.

Velocity-Displacement

v² = v₀² + 2as

Final velocity from initial velocity, acceleration, and displacement.

Average Velocity

s = ½(v + v₀)t

Displacement from the average of initial and final velocities over time.

Pressure, Force & Area

F
P A

Pressure (P)

P = F / A

Unit: Pascals (Pa)

Force (F)

F = P × A

Unit: Newtons (N)

Area (A)

A = F / P

Unit: Meters Squared (m²)

Mechanical Advantage

Simple Machines

Devices that change a force's direction or magnitude.
Examples: Lever, pulley, inclined plane, wedge, screw, wheel & axle.

Compound Machines

A combination of two or more simple machines working together.
Example: A pair of scissors combines two levers.

27 Safety Procedures & Guidelines (SPG)

  1. Always wear safety goggles.
  2. Know safety equipment locations.
  3. No eating or drinking in the lab.
  4. Tie back long hair securely.
  5. Avoid loose clothing or jewelry.
  6. Handle all chemicals with care.
  7. Never taste or directly smell chemicals.
  8. Read all chemical labels carefully.
  9. Report all accidents immediately.
  1. Dispose of chemical waste properly.
  2. Keep your workspace clean & tidy.
  3. Handle glassware with caution.
  4. Use a fume hood for volatile fumes.
  5. Never work alone in the laboratory.
  6. Wash hands before leaving the lab.
  7. Follow all instructions precisely.
  8. Never point test tubes at anyone.
  9. Handle hot equipment with tongs.
  1. Check glassware for cracks or chips.
  2. Add acid to water, never the reverse.
  3. Use equipment only as intended.
  4. Secure gas cylinders when in use.
  5. Know all emergency procedures.
  6. Wear appropriate protective gloves.
  7. Use a pipette filler, never by mouth.
  8. Clearly label all containers.
  9. Treat all chemicals as hazardous.
